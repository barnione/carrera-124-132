Controls and switches

Some buttons carry two functions. Any programming step can be cancelled with ESC / PACE CAR; settings are confirmed with ENTER / START.

2

First power-up

  1. Connect the track's connecting rail and the transformer to the Control Unit.
  2. Plug wired speed controllers into the numbered connectors. A WIRELESS+ receiver must go into connector 1; extra wired controllers in connectors 3 and 4 take addresses 5 and 6.
  3. Slide the power switch to ON. After about one second the centre LED lights permanently and a short beep sounds. The unit is ready.
3

Coding a car to a speed controller

Required before a car's first race, and whenever you want a different controller to drive it. Settings stay saved in the car until it is recoded.

  1. Switch the Control Unit on and place the car on the track.
  2. Press CODE once. The first LED lights up.
  3. Press the lane-change button once on the controller that should drive this car.
  4. The car's lights (if fitted) flash while LEDs 2–4 light in sequence.
  5. When the centre LED lights permanently and a tone sounds, coding is complete.
  6. Repeat for each car/controller pair, one car at a time.
WarningKeep only the car being coded on the rails. With two uncoded cars on the track the unit cannot tell them apart.
4

Setting basic speed

Per car, 10 levels. The 5 LEDs show the level: a flashing LED counts as a half step, a solid LED as a full step. Useful for evening out the field.

  1. With the unit on, place the car(s) to adjust on the track (already coded per sec. 3).
  2. Press SPEED once; the LEDs show the stored level.
  3. Press SPEED repeatedly to step from level 1 (slowest) to 10 (fastest).
  4. Confirm with ENTER / START. A short running light plus the centre LED confirms the save.
5

Setting braking effect

  1. With the unit on, place the car(s) to adjust on the track.
  2. Press BRAKE once; the LEDs show the stored level.
  3. Press BRAKE repeatedly: level 1 = long coasting, level 10 = strong braking.
  4. Confirm with ENTER / START.
NoteSpeed, brake and fuel are stored in the car, not in the track, so the car keeps its settings on any Digital 124/132 circuit.
6

Setting fuel tank capacity

Applies to all cars simultaneously. Only relevant with the Pit Stop lane; without it, leave the fuel switch off.

  1. Slide the fuel switch to ON to activate the fuelling function.
  2. Place the car(s) on the track and press FUEL once; the LEDs show the last-used capacity.
  3. Press FUEL repeatedly: level 1 = smallest tank, level 10 = full tank.
  4. Confirm with ENTER / START.
RefuellingA low tank makes the car's lights flash and the car slow down or stutter. Enter the Pit Stop and release the throttle: waiting until the lights stop flashing fills the tank; leaving earlier fills it partially.
7

Pace Car

One car can be registered as the Pace Car. It leads the field at a fixed speed, changes lanes on its own, then pulls into the pits. Requires a Pit Stop lane.

  1. Switch the Control Unit on and place only the intended Pace Car on the track.
  2. Press CODE three times. The first three LEDs light up.
  3. Press the lane-change button on a speed controller once. LEDs 2 to 5 light in sequence; wait until the centre LED lights again.
  4. Squeeze the throttle until the car reaches the desired cruising speed, then press the lane-change button once more. Encoding is complete and the car enters the Pit Stop lane on its own during the next laps.
  5. During a race, press ESC / PACE CAR once to release it: LEDs 2 and 3 light and the car leaves the pits. Press the button again and it re-enters the Pit Lane within the current lap.
NoteOn a Position Tower the Pace Car is shown as address 8, the autonomous car as address 7. To enable this display: switch the unit off, hold BRAKE, switch it on, release; press BRAKE to toggle (1 LED = off, 2 LEDs = on) and confirm with ENTER / START.
8

Autonomous (ghost) car

A self-driving opponent at a constant speed.

  1. Switch the Control Unit on and place only the car to be programmed on the track.
  2. Press CODE twice. The first two LEDs on the Control Unit light up.
  3. Press the lane-change button on a speed controller once. LEDs 3 to 5 light in sequence; wait until the centre LED lights again.
  4. Squeeze that controller's throttle until the car reaches the desired speed, then press the lane-change button once more. Encoding is complete; the car now drives on its own at that speed.
  5. To decode: run the normal coding sequence from sec. 3, which overwrites the autonomous programming. The setting is otherwise kept permanently.
9

Factory reset · energy saving

  1. Reset: switch the unit off. Hold ESC / PACE CAR, switch the unit back on, release the button. Speed, brake, fuel, sound and lap counting return to factory values. Cars keep their own stored settings unless recoded.
  2. Energy saving: after ~20 minutes idle, tower/displays/start light switch off. To wake it, briefly squeeze any controller's throttle, press a lane-change button, or press any key on the Control Unit; all settings are kept.
